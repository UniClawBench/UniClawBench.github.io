BRAND VOICE GUIDELINES
======================

Our voice is direct, warm, and confident. We assume our reader is a smart adult
who values clarity over cleverness.

Write like you talk
-------------------
- Use contractions. "We'll" not "We will." "Don't" not "Do not."
- Read your draft out loud. If you stumble, the reader will too.
- Start sentences with conjunctions when it helps the rhythm. But don't overdo it.

Be specific
-----------
- "Faster" is not specific. "Three times faster than the previous version" is.
- Use real numbers, real customer names (with permission), real screenshots.
- If you cannot be specific, ask whether the claim is worth making.

Avoid
-----
- "Game-changing," "revolutionary," "delightful," "frictionless." These have
  been emptied of meaning by overuse.
- Long, hedge-y sentences. If you find yourself writing "we believe that, in
  many cases, our customers may experience…" — start over.
- Internal jargon in customer-facing copy. The customer does not know what
  the "platform layer" is.

Punctuation
-----------
- Em dashes are great. Use them.
- Avoid semicolons in customer-facing copy; they make the prose feel formal.
- Oxford commas: yes.

When in doubt
-------------
Ask the brand team in #brand-voice. Or send a draft to one of the brand
reviewers — Maya, Tomas, or Sara — for a second read.
